# Emergency Response Procedure Reference

## Domain-Specific Inputs Required

- Facility types and locations (office, warehouse, manufacturing, data center, fleet)
- Local emergency services contacts and response times
- On-site medical capabilities (AED locations, first aid kits, trained responders)
- Emergency notification system (mass notification vendor, PA system, two-way radio)
- Assembly areas and capacity
- Shelter-in-place locations identified
- Hazardous materials on-site (if any)
- Business continuity plan exists (yes/no, reference document)
- Emergency power and life safety systems inventory
- Persons requiring evacuation assistance (general provisions, not named individuals)
- Local hazard profile (tornado, earthquake, flood, wildfire, hurricane, winter storm)

## Key Procedure Provisions to Include

### Emergency Classification Levels
Define a tiered response:
- **Level 1 - Localized**: Single area affected, managed with on-site resources (small spill, minor medical, isolated alarm)
- **Level 2 - Facility-Wide**: Building evacuation or shelter-in-place, external emergency services requested
- **Level 3 - Campus/Multi-Site**: Multiple facilities affected, crisis management team activated, potential business continuity impact

### Emergency Types and Response Protocols
Each emergency type gets its own sub-procedure:

**Fire/Explosion**
- Alarm activation procedures (manual pull, automatic detection)
- Evacuation routes and assembly areas
- Floor warden / sweep responsibilities
- Fire suppression system overview (sprinkler, clean agent, etc.)
- Re-entry authorization process

**Medical Emergency**
- Call 911 / local emergency number
- AED and first aid responder deployment
- Scene safety assessment
- Information to provide to EMS
- Post-incident documentation
- [USER INPUT REQUIRED: Good Samaritan policy reference]

**Severe Weather**
- Monitoring and alert sources (NWS, commercial weather service)
- Shelter-in-place locations by facility
- Tornado: Interior rooms, lowest floor, away from windows
- Earthquake: Drop/Cover/Hold, post-quake building assessment
- Flood: Evacuation triggers and routes
- Winter storm: Early dismissal decision authority

**Active Threat / Active Shooter**
- Run / Hide / Fight framework (or organization-preferred model)
- Law enforcement notification (911 + internal security)
- Lockdown procedures
- Communication protocols during event
- Reunification procedures post-event
- [USER INPUT REQUIRED: Reference to Workplace Violence Prevention Policy]

**Hazardous Material Release**
- Isolation and containment (if trained and safe to do so)
- Evacuation vs. shelter-in-place decision criteria
- SDS location and access
- HAZMAT team notification
- Decontamination procedures
- Regulatory reporting requirements (EPA, OSHA, local fire marshal)

**Utility Failure**
- Power outage: Emergency lighting, generator activation, UPS systems
- Water/plumbing failure: Restroom alternatives, fire suppression impact
- HVAC failure: Temperature thresholds for evacuation
- Telecommunications failure: Alternate communication methods

**Bomb Threat**
- Cross-reference to standalone Bomb Threat Response Procedure

### Evacuation Procedures
- Primary and secondary evacuation routes posted and maintained
- Assembly area assignments by building/floor
- Accountability process (headcount, check-in system)
- Persons requiring assistance: Pre-identified buddy system or evacuation chairs
- Visitor and contractor accountability
- Do not re-enter until authorized by [USER INPUT REQUIRED: authority, e.g., Fire Marshal, Security Director]

### Shelter-in-Place Procedures
- Triggers for shelter-in-place vs. evacuation
- Designated shelter locations
- Sealing procedures for chemical/biological events
- Communication during shelter-in-place
- Duration expectations and re-assessment triggers

### Crisis Communication
- Internal notification chain: who notifies whom, in what order
- Mass notification system activation authority
- External communication: Media, regulators, families
- Designated spokesperson: [USER INPUT REQUIRED: role/title]
- Social media monitoring during crisis

### Post-Incident
- After-action review (AAR) required for all Level 2 and Level 3 events
- Incident documentation and evidence preservation
- Employee support and counseling resources (EAP)
- Regulatory reporting timelines (OSHA, EPA, local authorities)
- Insurance notification
- Corrective action tracking

## Compliance Metrics

- Evacuation drill completion rate (target: 100% of scheduled drills)
- Average evacuation time vs. target time
- Assembly area accountability rate (percentage of personnel accounted for)
- After-action review completion rate for Level 2+ events
- Employee emergency training completion rate
- Time from incident to mass notification activation
- Corrective action closure rate from AAR findings

## Common Legal Considerations

- OSHA 1910.38: Written EAP required if >10 employees; must include evacuation procedures, reporting procedures, employee notification, and training
- OSHA 1910.39: Fire prevention plan required where 1910.38 applies
- ADA: Evacuation plans must accommodate persons with disabilities
- Local fire codes: Drill frequency, occupancy limits, fire watch requirements
- State-specific emergency planning requirements (e.g., California IIPP)
- Workers compensation: Injuries during drills or actual emergencies
