# Incident Reporting Policy Reference

- Incident management system/platform (e.g., Resolver, D3 Security, i-Sight, internal)
- Incident classification taxonomy (or adopt the one below)
- Reporting channels (phone, email, app, web portal, walk-in)

## Key Policy Provisions to Include

### Incident Classification

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Security** | Unauthorized access, theft, vandalism, trespassing, suspicious activity |
| **Safety** | Workplace injury, slip/fall, equipment malfunction, near-miss |
| **Threat** | Verbal/written threat, stalking, intimidation, bomb threat |
| **Environmental** | Hazmat spill, air quality, water contamination |
| **Information** | Data breach, lost device, unauthorized disclosure, social engineering |
| **Workplace Violence** | Physical altercation, domestic violence spillover, active threat |
| **Executive/VIP** | Threat to executive, protest at executive residence, travel incident |
| **Natural Event** | Weather damage, earthquake, flood, fire |
| **Reputational** | Media inquiry, social media threat, brand impersonation |

### Severity Levels

- **Critical (S1)**: Life safety threat, active incident, significant asset loss, regulatory breach. Immediate response. Notify executive leadership within [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., 1 hour].
- **High (S2)**: Potential for harm or significant loss, requires same-day response and investigation. Notify security leadership within [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., 4 hours].
- **Medium (S3)**: No immediate threat but requires investigation. Response within [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., 24 hours].
- **Low (S4)**: Informational, minor, or administrative. Documented for trend analysis. Response within [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., 72 hours].

### Reporting Requirements

**Who Must Report:**
All employees, contractors, vendors, and visitors shall report security incidents immediately upon discovery or observation.

**What Must Be Reported:**
- Any incident matching the classification taxonomy above
- Near-misses and suspicious activity (even if no harm occurred)
- Any situation where an employee feels unsafe

**How to Report:**
- Emergency (life safety): Call 911, then notify Security at [USER INPUT REQUIRED: number]
- Urgent: Contact Security Operations Center at [USER INPUT REQUIRED: 24/7 number]
- Non-urgent: Submit via [USER INPUT REQUIRED: system/portal/email]
- Anonymous: [USER INPUT REQUIRED: hotline number or portal]

**Minimum Information to Capture:**
- Date, time, and location of incident
- Description of what happened
- Persons involved (names, roles, or descriptions)
- Witnesses
- Injuries or property damage
- Actions taken at the scene
- Evidence collected or preserved

### Investigation Process
1. Initial report received and logged
2. Severity classification assigned
3. Investigator assigned based on category and severity
4. Scene secured and evidence preserved (if applicable)
5. Witness interviews conducted
6. Root cause analysis completed
7. Findings documented
8. Corrective actions recommended
9. Report reviewed by security leadership
10. Case closed with documented disposition

### Evidence Handling
- Physical evidence: Photograph in place, collect with gloves, label, secure in evidence storage
- Digital evidence: Screenshot, preserve logs, do not modify original files
- Video evidence: Export and secure copy, preserve original recording
- Chain of custody form required for all physical evidence

## Compliance Metrics

- Average time from incident to initial report
- Average time from report to investigator assignment
- Investigation closure rate within SLA by severity level
- Corrective action implementation rate
- Percentage of incidents with complete initial reports (all required fields)
- Year-over-year incident trend by category and severity
- Employee reporting training completion rate

## Common Legal Considerations

- Attorney-client privilege: Label investigation reports appropriately when directed by legal
- Whistleblower protections: Non-retaliation for good-faith reporting
- OSHA reporting timelines are legally mandated, not discretionary
- Workers compensation: Incident reports may become evidence in claims
- Privacy: Incident reports containing PII must be handled per data protection policies
- Records retention: Incident records retention per legal and regulatory requirements
