# Bomb Threat Response Procedure Reference

## Domain-Specific Inputs Required

- Facility threat profile (public-facing, corporate, R&D, government-adjacent)
- Law enforcement liaison contacts (local PD bomb squad, FBI field office, ATF)
- Mail screening capabilities (X-ray, chemical detection, trained personnel)
- Search team composition (security, facilities, department reps)
- Decision authority for evacuation (who makes the call)
- Bomb threat checklist availability (physical copies at reception, SOC, etc.)
- Package/mail receiving locations and procedures
- History of threats to organization or industry

## Key Procedure Provisions to Include

### Threat Receipt Protocols

**Telephonic Threats**
- Keep caller on the line as long as possible
- Signal a coworker to notify security immediately
- Complete the Bomb Threat Checklist during or immediately after the call (see Appendix)
- Record exact words used by caller
- Note: caller's voice characteristics, background noises, caller ID information
- Do not hang up first; if caller disconnects, do not use that phone line again until released by investigators
- Transfer or conference to security/SOC if technically possible without disconnecting

**Written Threats (Letter, Note)**
- Do not handle further once threat is recognized
- Place in a plastic bag or leave undisturbed
- Note who handled it and when
- Preserve envelope and any packaging
- Photograph in place before moving

**Email / Electronic Threats**
- Do not delete, forward, or reply
- Screenshot immediately
- Note sender information, headers if visible, time received
- Notify security and IT simultaneously
- Preserve digital evidence chain

**Social Media Threats**
- Screenshot with URL, timestamp, and account information
- Do not engage with the poster
- Notify security, legal, and corporate communications
- Report to platform and law enforcement

### Threat Assessment

Security shall conduct an immediate threat assessment considering:
- Specificity of the threat (named target, time, location, device description)
- Credibility indicators (insider knowledge, prior threats, current threat landscape)
- Context (labor disputes, terminations, public controversy, industry targeting)
- Concurrent intelligence (other threats, suspicious activity reports)

Assessment outcomes:
- **Non-Credible**: Document, monitor, no physical response. Notify law enforcement for documentation.
- **Indeterminate**: Initiate search of threatened area, prepare for possible evacuation, notify law enforcement
- **Credible**: Evacuate immediately, notify law enforcement, establish perimeter

### Search Procedures

If a search is authorized:
- Search teams work in pairs
- Search assigned areas systematically (floor to waist, waist to ceiling, ceiling and above)
- Searchers look for items that are: out of place, not recognized by area occupants, suspicious in appearance
- **DO NOT** touch, move, or open suspicious items
- **DO NOT** use two-way radios or cell phones within [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., 300 feet] of a suspicious item (RF-initiated device risk)
- Report findings to search coordinator immediately
- If nothing found, report "area clear" to coordinator

### Suspicious Package/Mail Indicators
- Unexpected package with no return address or unfamiliar return address
- Excessive postage, restrictive markings ("Personal," "Confidential")
- Misspelled words, incorrect titles, generic addressing
- Unusual weight, size, shape, or rigidity
- Stains, discoloration, strange odors
- Protruding wires, foil, or visible components
- Ticking or humming sounds
- Excessive tape or wrapping

### Suspicious Package Response
1. Do not open, shake, or empty contents
2. Do not smell, touch, or taste any substance
3. Place on stable surface if being carried; otherwise leave in place
4. Clear the immediate area (minimum [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., 300 feet] radius)
5. Notify security and 911
6. Do not use cell phones or radios near the item
7. Wash hands thoroughly if package was handled

- After-action review within [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., 72 hours]

### Appendix: Bomb Threat Checklist

Include a printable one-page checklist with fields for:

**Call Information:**
- Date/time of call
- Phone number/line call received on
- Duration of call
- Exact words of threat

**Questions to Ask (if safe and possible):**
- When will it explode/detonate?
- Where is it right now?
- What does it look like?
- What kind of bomb is it?
- What will cause it to explode?
- Did you place it?
- Why?
- What is your name?
- Where are you calling from?

**Caller Characteristics:**
- Gender, estimated age
- Accent, speech pattern (fast, slow, stutter, lisp)
- Emotional state (calm, angry, excited, rational, irrational)
- Background sounds (traffic, music, machinery, voices, PA system)
- Familiar voice? Who does it sound like?

## Compliance Metrics

- Bomb threat checklist availability at all reception/SOC points (target: 100%)
- Employee awareness training completion rate
- Average time from threat receipt to security notification
- Tabletop exercise completion (annual minimum)
- After-action review completion rate for actual threats

## Common Legal Considerations

- False bomb threats are federal crimes (18 USC 1038) and state crimes in all 50 states
- Duty to report threats to law enforcement (organizational policy decision; some jurisdictions may mandate)
- Evidence preservation for law enforcement investigation
- Employee communication must balance transparency with investigation integrity
- Workers compensation for injuries during evacuation
- Liability for failure to evacuate vs. unnecessary evacuation (document decision rationale)
