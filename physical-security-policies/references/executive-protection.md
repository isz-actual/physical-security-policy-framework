# Executive Protection Policy Reference

## Domain-Specific Inputs Required

- Protectees (CEO, C-suite, board members, visiting VIPs -- by role, not name)
- Threat landscape for organization and industry
- Existing protective intelligence program
- EP team composition (in-house, contract, hybrid)
- Armed or unarmed protection
- Residential security measures in place
- Travel program (domestic, international, both)
- Executive family inclusion in program (yes/no, scope)
- Vehicle fleet (armored, standard, both)
- Corporate aviation program (if applicable)
- Integration with event security
- Social media exposure management
- Budget authorization level

## Key Policy Provisions to Include

### Program Authorization and Scope
- Authorized by: [USER INPUT REQUIRED: Board, CEO, CISO, General Counsel]
- Protectee criteria: Based on threat assessment, role criticality, and board direction
- Scope includes:
  - Protective intelligence and threat monitoring
  - Close protection (when warranted by threat level)
  - Secure transportation
  - Residential security assessment
  - Travel security (advance work, route planning, emergency extraction)
  - Event security coordination
  - Family protection (if authorized)

### Threat Level Framework
- **Level 1 -- Baseline**: Standard protective intelligence monitoring, no close protection. Periodic threat briefs.
- **Level 2 -- Elevated**: Enhanced monitoring, travel advance work required, secure transportation arranged.
- **Level 3 -- Heightened**: Close protection detail assigned, residential security enhanced, all travel with EP.
- **Level 4 -- Imminent**: Maximum protection posture, potential relocation, law enforcement coordination, board notification.

Threat level determined by: Security Director in consultation with protective intelligence team and outside threat assessment consultants as needed.

### Protective Intelligence
- Open source intelligence (OSINT) monitoring of threats to protectees and organization
- Social media monitoring for direct and indirect threats
- Monitoring of protest activity, activist groups, and issue-based threats
- Coordination with law enforcement intelligence units
- Threat assessment and management (cross-reference Workplace Violence Prevention Policy)
- Pre-travel intelligence briefings
- Event venue threat assessment
- Quarterly threat landscape briefing to protectees

### Close Protection Operations
When close protection is authorized:
- Detail composition and shift structure
- Protective formations and movement protocols
- Communication protocols (radio, coded language)
- Medical preparedness (first aid, AED, trauma kit, hospital routes)
- Contingency planning:
  - Vehicle attack
  - Armed assault
  - Crowd surge
  - Medical emergency
  - Vehicle breakdown/accident
  - Protest encounter
- Advance work requirements:
  - Venue survey
  - Route planning (primary, secondary, emergency)
  - Hospital and safe haven identification
  - Local law enforcement notification and coordination
  - Counter-surveillance

### Secure Transportation
- Vehicle selection criteria (armored when threat warrants)
- Driver qualifications and training (defensive/evasive driving)
- Route planning methodology
- Vehicle inspection procedures (pre-departure sweep)
- GPS monitoring and communication
- Arrival and departure protocols

### Residential Security
- Residential security assessment (annual minimum, or upon threat escalation)
- Assessment covers:
  - Physical security (locks, lighting, cameras, alarm)
  - Perimeter assessment
  - Neighborhood threat profile
  - Emergency response access and timing
  - Communication and alarm monitoring
  - Family safety briefing
- Recommendations documented with implementation tracking
- [USER INPUT REQUIRED: Company funding policy for residential security improvements]

### Travel Security
**Domestic:**
- Pre-travel threat assessment for destination
- Hotel security criteria
- Ground transportation arrangements
- Emergency contact card

**International:**
- Country risk assessment (use [USER INPUT REQUIRED: provider, e.g., WorldAware, Global Guardian, Crisis24])
- Pre-travel briefing required for medium and high-risk destinations
- In-country security support (meet and greet, secure transport, close protection)
- Communication plan (satellite phone for high-risk)
- Emergency extraction plan
- Medical evacuation insurance
- Passport and document security
- Device security (burner phone/laptop for high-risk destinations)

### Information Security for Protectees
- Travel itinerary distribution limited to need-to-know
- Public calendar scrubbing (no location details in public-facing calendars)
- Social media guidance for protectees and family
- Personal information protection (home address, family details, routines)
- Mail screening at office and residence (if authorized)

### Coordination with Other Functions
- Events team: Security requirements for public appearances
- Corporate communications: Media interaction security
- Legal: Restraining orders, threat prosecution
- HR: Employee threats involving executives
- IT: Cyber-physical threat convergence, executive device security

## Compliance Metrics

- Protective intelligence report timeliness and completeness
- Advance work completion rate for all protected travel
- Training currency for EP team (defensive driving, medical, firearms if applicable)
- Residential security assessment completion rate (annual)
- Threat case management closure rate
- Protectee satisfaction feedback (annual survey)
- Incident-free travel rate

## Common Legal Considerations

- Concealed carry licensing and reciprocity (state-by-state for armed details)
- International firearms laws (generally prohibit -- use licensed local providers)
- Privacy laws: Monitoring of threats may intersect with surveillance laws
- Use of force: EP agents subject to same legal standards as any person; no special authority
- Duty of care: Organization has duty of care for traveling executives
- International: FCPA considerations for government coordination abroad
- Insurance: Ensure EP activities covered under corporate liability policy
