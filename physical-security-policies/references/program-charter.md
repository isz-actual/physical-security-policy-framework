# Security Program Charter Reference

## Domain-Specific Inputs Required

- Reporting structure (Security reports to: CEO, COO, CLO, CISO, VP of Operations)
- Security department organizational chart
- Budget authority and approval chain
- Governance committee structure (Security Committee, Risk Committee, Board oversight)
- Existing risk management framework
- Regulatory environment driving security requirements
- Relationship with information security / cybersecurity program
- Insurance requirements driving security program elements

## Key Charter Provisions to Include

### Program Mission
Define the security program's mission in one to two sentences. It should address:
- Protection of people, assets, information, and reputation
- Alignment with organizational mission and risk tolerance
- Enabling business operations (not just restricting them)

### Program Scope
The physical security program encompasses:
- All owned, leased, and operated facilities
- All employees, contractors, visitors, and vendors while on company premises
- Company-sponsored events regardless of location
- Executive protection program
- Travel security
- Investigations (physical security related)
- Security technology systems
- Guard force management
- Emergency preparedness and response
- Liaison with law enforcement and intelligence agencies

Explicitly note what is NOT in scope (to avoid gaps and conflicts):
- Cybersecurity / information security (separate program, cross-referenced)
- Environmental health and safety (separate program, coordinate on emergency response)

### Governance Structure

**Security Director / CSO:**
- Overall program accountability
- Policy approval authority
- Budget management
- Executive reporting
- External agency liaison

**Security Operations:**
- 24/7 monitoring and response
- Guard force management
- Incident management
- Access control administration

**Protective Intelligence:**
- Threat assessment and management
- Executive protection
- Travel security
- OSINT monitoring

**Security Technology:**
- Access control systems
- Video surveillance
- Alarm systems
- Security technology roadmap

**Investigations:**
- Internal investigations (physical security)
- Coordination with HR, Legal, and law enforcement
- Evidence management
