# Visitor Management Policy Reference

## Domain-Specific Inputs Required

- Visitor management system (electronic kiosk, manual log, cloud-based platform)
- Pre-registration process and system
- Visitor badge type (printed, pre-printed, reusable with tracking)
- Photo capture at check-in (yes/no, legal review needed)
- NDA or security acknowledgment required at sign-in (yes/no)
- Escort requirements by zone
- Visitor Wi-Fi policy (separate SSID, registration, acceptable use)
- VIP/Board visitor handling procedures
- Facility tour protocols
- Loading dock and delivery driver visitor classification

## Key Policy Provisions to Include

### Visitor Categories
- **Scheduled Business Visitor**: Pre-registered, sponsored by an employee
- **Walk-In Visitor**: Unscheduled, requires real-time authorization
- **Contractor/Vendor**: Recurring access, separate credentialing process (cross-reference Access Control Policy)
- **VIP/Executive Guest**: Expedited check-in, designated escort, confidentiality protections
- **Government/Regulatory Inspector**: Verify credentials, notify legal and security, escort to relevant areas only
- **Delivery Personnel**: Limited to loading dock/mailroom, no general facility access without escort
- **Emergency Services**: Law enforcement, fire, EMS -- verify identity, provide access as needed, notify management

### Pre-Registration
- Host employee submits visitor request [USER INPUT REQUIRED: timeframe, e.g., 24 hours] in advance
- Required information: visitor full name, company, purpose of visit, date/time, host employee
- Pre-registration triggers:
  - Badge pre-printing (if applicable)
  - Lobby notification to reception
  - Parking reservation (if applicable)
  - NDA pre-send for signature (if required)

### Check-In Process
1. Visitor presents government-issued photo ID
2. Receptionist or kiosk verifies against pre-registration list
3. Photo captured (if policy permits) and badge printed
4. Visitor signs acknowledgment form (safety rules, NDA, photography restrictions)
5. Host employee notified and required to meet visitor at reception
6. Visitor badge must be visibly worn at all times

### Escort Requirements
- Zone 1 (Public): No escort required
- Zone 2 (General Employee): Escort recommended, host responsible
- Zone 3 (Restricted): Escort mandatory at all times, no unattended visitor access
- Zone 4 (High Security): Pre-approved visitor list only, continuous escort by cleared personnel

### During Visit
- Visitor must remain with host or designated escort
- Photography, recording, and personal device use: [USER INPUT REQUIRED: restricted/prohibited in certain areas]
- Visitor Wi-Fi access provided (no corporate network access)
- Visitor must not access unlocked workstations, open documents, or proprietary information

### Check-Out Process
1. Visitor returns badge to reception or designated drop point
2. Check-out logged in visitor management system
3. Unreturned badges flagged within [USER INPUT REQUIRED: e.g., end of business day]
4. Unreturned badges deactivated immediately (if electronic)

### Denied Entry
Conditions for denying visitor access:
- No valid photo ID
- Not pre-registered and host unavailable to authorize
- On denied persons list (cross-reference with security watch list)
- Refuses to sign acknowledgment form
- Intoxication or threatening behavior
- All denials documented and reported to security

## Compliance Metrics

- Pre-registration compliance rate (percentage of visitors pre-registered vs. walk-in)
- Badge return rate (target: 100%)
- Average check-in processing time
- Escort compliance rate for restricted zones
- Denied entry incidents per quarter
- Visitor log completeness (all required fields captured)

## Common Legal Considerations

- Photo ID requirements: Verify applicable state/local laws regarding mandatory ID for building entry
- Photo capture: State biometric and privacy laws may apply (Illinois BIPA, GDPR)
- NDA enforceability: Ensure legal review of visitor acknowledgment forms
- Government inspector access: Cannot unreasonably delay or deny access to authorized regulatory inspectors
- ADA: Visitor check-in process must be accessible
- Data retention: Visitor logs and photos must have defined retention and destruction schedule
- Union/CBA: Union representatives may have specific access rights
